## AIM

To maintain and improve the health of patients, securing the greatest possible health gain from available resources, and ensuring all care is delivered safely, effectively, responsively and caringly — through a governance process that means the service is **well led**.

---

## POLICY

1. **Clinical governance is the framework through which `[CLINIC NAME]` is accountable for continuously improving the quality of its services** and safeguarding high standards of care.

2. **Clinical effectiveness** is thinking critically about what we do, questioning whether it achieves the intended result, and changing practice accordingly — based on evidence of what works.

3. Clinical governance ensures **the right person is doing the right thing (evidence-based practice), in the right way (skills and competence), in the right place, at the right time, with the right result.**

4. **The components:**

   | | |
   |---|---|
   | Clinical effectiveness and research | Evidence-based practice, NICE guidance |
   | Clinical audit | C033 |
   | Risk management and incidents | C017 |
   | Education and training | C010 |
   | Patient involvement and experience | C04, patient feedback |
   | Information and IT | C03, C03e, C09 |
   | Human resources | C025, C010 |

5. **Governance is not a meeting — it is what the meeting does.** A committee that receives reports and takes no decisions provides no assurance.

### How decisions are recorded

Every item results in one of: **noted** · **action, with a named owner and a date** · **escalated** · **closed**.

**Actions are carried forward until complete.** An action list that resets each meeting is not a governance system.

## ASSURANCE

**Governance requires evidence, not assertion.** For each area, the meeting should be able to answer: *how do we know?*

| Claim | Evidence |
|---|---|
| "Our record keeping is good" | The last record keeping audit, its findings, and the re-audit |
| "Staff are trained" | The training matrix, with no overdue entries |
| "We learn from complaints" | Actions from complaints, completed, with dates |
| "Our prescribing is safe" | Prescribing audit against formulary or guideline |
| "We are safe to practise" | Registration and indemnity checks, in date, for everyone |

## What changed from C024 v01 — and why

v01 stated the principles of clinical governance correctly. The seven components, the definition of clinical effectiveness, and the *right person / right thing / right way* framing are all sound and are retained, as is the cross-reference list to the other policies — which is one of the more useful features of the document.

Three changes.

### 1. It described clinical governance without describing the governance meeting

This is the substantive gap, and it is the same one found in **C015**.

v01 refers throughout to *"a robust clinical governance committee"* and lists it as the mechanism. It does not say who chairs it, who attends, how often it meets, what quorum is, what it discusses, how decisions are recorded, or what happens to actions.

That matters more here than anywhere else, because **nearly every policy in the corpus ends by reporting something to the governance committee.** Incidents, complaints, audit findings, safeguarding activity, training compliance, policy reviews, candour cases — all of them terminate at a body that no document defines. If the meeting is not real, none of those reporting lines are.

v02 adds terms of reference, a **standing agenda built from what the other policies actually send to it**, and the rule that an item results in a decision — noted, actioned with an owner and a date, escalated, or closed — with actions carried forward until complete.

### 2. There was no annual cycle and no assurance framework

v01 lists what clinical governance covers. It does not say when anything happens, or how the clinic would know whether any of it is working.

Added: an annual governance calendar, and an assurance table that pairs each governance claim with the evidence that supports it. The distinction matters at inspection — *"our record keeping is good"* is an assertion; *"here is the audit, the actions and the re-audit"* is assurance, and only the second one survives the follow-up question.

### 3. References

- **"CQC: Prof Sparrow No4 Quality Improvement Activity"** — the **fourth** appearance of this source in the corpus, after C01 Chaperone, C07 Medicines Management and C040 Remote Consultations. It is presented as CQC material and is not; it cannot be verified, reviewed or updated
- *GMC Good Medical Practice 2013* → 2024
- **No GPhC.** Governance is **Pharmacy Principle 1 (governance)**, and for a pharmacist-led service it is the directly applicable standard
- **Regulation 17** — the regulation this policy exists to satisfy — was not cited
- The cross-reference list runs *"Manual Handling Policy Infection Control Policy"* together as a single item, and refers to the *"Whistleblowing Policy"*, now superseded by **S03 Speaking Up v02**

### Addition

**"Nothing to report" is itself a data point.** The standing agenda is covered whether or not there is anything to say, because a quarter with no incidents, no complaints and no concerns raised in a busy service usually means they are not reaching the meeting — not that nothing happened. That reasoning now appears in three policies (S03, C017 and here), and it is the same underlying control.
