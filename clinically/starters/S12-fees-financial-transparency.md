## AIM

Patients at `[CLINIC NAME]` pay for their care. A patient who does not fully understand what they are agreeing to pay, and when, has not given properly informed consent — consent to treatment and consent to the cost of it are not separable in a private service.

This policy sets out how patients are told what things cost, before they commit; what happens when the cost changes mid-course; and how we handle refunds, disputes, and the circumstances in which a patient stops paying.

It exists to make a straightforward promise enforceable: **no patient at `[CLINIC NAME]` is ever presented with a charge they were not told about in advance.**

## POLICY

1. **Fees are published before contact, not on arrival.** A current price list is displayed `[CONFIRM: where — default: on the website, in the waiting area, and provided on request]`. It states clearly what is and is not included.

2. **The patient is told the cost of their specific care before they consent to it.** A published list is a starting point, not sufficient on its own. Before assessment or treatment begins, the patient is told what *their* pathway will cost, including any elements the headline price does not cover.

3. **Where the total cost cannot be known in advance, we say so, and give a range.** Titration-based, session-based, or investigation-dependent care is quoted as "£X for the initial assessment, then £Y per follow-up; most patients need between A and B follow-ups." A single up-front figure implying finality, where none exists, is not permitted.

4. **The following must be stated explicitly and never assumed:**
   - Whether the price of a consultation includes the cost of any medicine
   - Whether prescriptions are issued as private prescriptions the patient dispenses and pays for elsewhere, and roughly what that costs
   - Whether investigations, blood tests, or scans are included or charged separately
   - Whether follow-up appointments are included, and for how long
   - Whether the fee is payable if the patient is assessed and found not suitable for treatment
   - `[CONFIRM: whether an assessment fee is refundable where no treatment is offered. Default: the assessment fee is retained, because the assessment was delivered — but this must be stated before the appointment, not after.]`

5. **Fees are agreed before treatment, in a recorded way.** `[CONFIRM: the mechanism. Default: the patient confirms the quoted fee in writing (booking confirmation, signed consent form, or e-form) and a copy is retained.]` A verbal quote with no record is not sufficient evidence that the patient was informed.

6. **Prices do not change part-way through an agreed course of treatment.** Where a patient has begun a defined course, the price agreed at the start applies to that course. Price changes take effect for new patients and for new courses, from a stated date.

7. **Where clinical need changes the cost, we stop and tell the patient before incurring it.** A clinician who identifies that more sessions, further investigation, or a different medicine is needed explains the clinical reason and the cost implication, and obtains agreement, before proceeding. Cost is not discovered at the point of invoice.

8. **No one is deterred from clinically urgent care by a payment process.** Where a patient presents with an urgent or emergency need, care is provided or the patient is safely directed to appropriate emergency services. Payment is dealt with afterwards. Clinical need takes precedence over payment status, without exception.

9. **Non-payment is a commercial matter and is handled entirely separately from clinical care.** Debt recovery is never conducted by clinical staff during a consultation, is never raised in a clinical setting in front of others, and never affects clinical decision-making, urgency of appointment, or willingness to provide safe care.

10. **Financial interests are declared.** Where a clinician or the organisation has a financial interest in a recommended treatment, product, onward referral, or supplier, that interest is declared to the patient. This includes recommending a medicine dispensed by an associated pharmacy. `[CONFIRM: known financial interests to be declared as standard.]`

11. **Payment of a fee never buys a clinical outcome.** No patient is offered, and no staff member may imply, that paying secures a particular diagnosis, a prescription, or a specific treatment. Where a clinician assesses that a treatment is not appropriate, the fee does not change that.

12. **We do not charge for making a complaint, for a copy of a patient's own record, or for a routine safeguarding or GP notification letter.** `[CONFIRM: charges for non-routine administrative work — e.g. medico-legal reports, insurance forms, employer letters. These must be listed with prices on the published fee list.]`

## PROCEDURE

### Before the appointment

1. Fees for the service are on the website and can be quoted by anyone taking bookings.
2. At booking, the patient is told the fee for the appointment they are booking, what it covers, and the cancellation terms.
3. The booking confirmation restates the fee in writing.
4. The patient is told what further costs are likely if treatment follows.

### At the appointment

1. Before assessment begins, the clinician or reception confirms the patient understands the fee and what it covers.
2. Where treatment is recommended, the clinician sets out the full expected cost of the pathway — sessions, medicines, monitoring, follow-up — before the patient consents.
3. The cost discussion is recorded in the clinical record alongside the consent entry.

### Cancellations, non-attendance and refunds

| Situation | Position |
|---|---|
| Patient cancels with `[CONFIRM: default 48 hours]` notice or more | `[CONFIRM: default — full refund or free reschedule]` |
| Patient cancels with less than `[CONFIRM: default 48 hours]` notice | `[CONFIRM: default — fee retained, discretion applied for illness, bereavement or emergency]` |
| Patient does not attend | `[CONFIRM: default — fee retained]` |
| `[CLINIC NAME]` cancels or the clinician is unavailable | Full refund or free reschedule, at the patient's choice, always |
| Patient assessed but not suitable for treatment | `[CONFIRM — see policy item 4]` |
| Patient stops treatment part-way through a pre-paid course | `[CONFIRM: default — refund of unused sessions less any non-recoverable costs already incurred, itemised]` |
| Service not delivered as described | Full refund, and the matter is logged as a complaint under C04 |

Cancellation and refund terms are given to the patient in writing at booking. They are not published only in a policy the patient never sees.

### Refund requests

1. Requests are made to `[CONFIRM: named role]` and logged.
2. Acknowledged within `[CONFIRM: default 3 working days]`.
3. Decided within `[CONFIRM: default 20 working days]`, with the reason given in writing.
4. Where a refund is refused, the patient is told how to complain, including their right to escalate to `[CONFIRM: e.g. ISCAS or the relevant ombudsman/ADR scheme, if a member]`.
5. Refund decisions are recorded and reviewed at `[CONFIRM: the governance meeting]` — a pattern of refund requests about the same service is a quality signal, not just a financial one.

### Fee disputes

A dispute about a fee is treated as a complaint under C04, with the same timescales and the same right of escalation. It is not handled informally and off the record because it concerns money.

### Reviewing the fee list

1. Reviewed `[CONFIRM: default annually]` by `[CONFIRM: role]`.
2. The version and effective date are recorded on the published list.
3. Patients mid-course are unaffected (policy item 6).
4. New prices take effect no sooner than `[CONFIRM: default 30 days]` after publication.

### Audit

`[CONFIRM: audit owner and frequency. Default: Registered Manager, annually.]` The audit asks:

- Is the published fee list current, and does it match what is actually charged?
- In a sample of records, is there evidence the patient was told the cost before consenting?
- Were there any charges applied that the patient had not been told about in advance?
- Are refund requests logged, decided within timescale, and reasoned?
- Are declared financial interests up to date?

## Why this policy exists — and why it wasn't in the 2022 set

There is no fees policy anywhere in the 123-document source corpus. That is a genuine gap, and a slightly surprising one for a private clinic, where every patient interaction involves a charge.

It surfaced at inspection. The CQC interview included the question **"how are people made aware of costs"** — which had no policy behind it. **Regulation 19 of the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014 is specifically about fees**, and a service that charges patients and has no fee policy has a visible hole in its Reg 19 evidence.

Three parts of this document deserve attention before adoption:

- **Item 8 (urgent care regardless of payment) and item 9 (non-payment kept away from clinical care).** These are the two provisions that protect against the most serious criticism a private provider can attract — that money affected a clinical decision. They should not be softened.

- **Item 3 (ranges where the total is unknowable).** This matters most for titration services such as ADHD, weight management, and hormone treatment, where the honest answer is a range, and a single up-front figure would mislead.

- **The `[CONFIRM]` at item 4 — is the assessment fee refundable if no treatment is offered?** This is the question most likely to generate a complaint. Whatever the clinic decides, the answer must be told to the patient *before* the appointment. Deciding it after the argument has started is the failure mode.
