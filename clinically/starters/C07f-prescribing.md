## Scope
All individuals in the employ of `[CLINIC NAME]` — meaning any person employed, self-employed, a volunteer, working under practising privileges, or under a contract of service with this establishment.

## AIM
To ensure that all patients are prescribed medication appropriately and safely, following a relevant consultation, by a prescriber working within their competence.

## POLICY
1. Only doctors and independent prescribers (pharmacists, nurses) registered with their regulator and working **within their own competence and declared scope of practice** may prescribe at `[CLINIC NAME]`.
2. Prescribing clinicians must use current published guidance — BNF, MIMS, NICE — in decision-making. **Doses, cautions, interactions and monitoring are taken from the BNF at the point of prescribing**, not from summaries.
3. `[CONFIRM: Does your clinic prescribe controlled drugs, and if so which schedules? **Default: NO — this clinic does not prescribe controlled drugs of any schedule.** If yes, C07a Controlled Drugs must also be in force.]`
4. `[CONFIRM: Does your clinic restrict prescribing to a defined formulary or exclude particular drug classes (e.g. antibiotics restricted to local guidance)? **Default: NO formal formulary — prescribing follows BNF/NICE and local antimicrobial guidance.** Record any restriction here so staff can follow it.]`
5. Unlicensed or off-label prescribing is undertaken only where clinically justified, in line with **GMC good practice in prescribing** and the prescriber's own regulator's standards, with the rationale recorded in the notes.
6. Every prescription follows a consultation in which the patient has been appropriately assessed. `[CONFIRM: Does your clinic issue any prescription without a same-day consultation? **Default: NO** — every supply follows a consultation or a documented structured review.]`
7. Remote prescribing follows the same standards as face-to-face. The prescriber must be satisfied they can make an adequate assessment; if not, the patient is offered a face-to-face appointment or is not prescribed for.

## PROCEDURE

### Before prescribing
Choice of medicine is patient-centred, taking into account:
age · patient choices · lifestyle · cultural and religious beliefs · allergies and intolerances · existing conditions and current medicines · previous adverse drug reactions · recommended prescribing regimens.

An up-to-date list of current medications and drug history is recorded in the notes at the first consultation and reviewed periodically.

### Information for the patient
Appropriate information about the medicine and its risks is given verbally and, wherever possible, in writing. `[CONFIRM: Does your clinic provide written medicine information — patient information leaflet, printed summary, or emailed? **Default: verbal plus the manufacturer's patient information leaflet.**]`

### Notifying the patient's own GP
Where the patient is registered with another GP (NHS or private), prescribing actions are shared with that GP.
`[CONFIRM: Is notifying the patient's NHS GP the default, with patient consent? **Default: YES — offered at every prescribing encounter and the patient's decision recorded in the notes.**]`

If the patient **declines** GP notification, the prescriber must consider whether it remains safe to prescribe, may limit the quantity or duration, and **records the decision and reasoning in the notes**. Repeat supplies may be refused where the patient continues to decline sharing.

### Monitoring and review
Prescribing clinicians review patients' medicines to ensure they remain appropriate and responsive to any change in condition. The effect of the medicine is monitored and action taken if a side effect or adverse reaction is observed, using the **Yellow Card scheme** where appropriate. Where a patient has difficulty taking medicines, support is offered (e.g. formulation change, compliance aids).

`[CONFIRM: Does your clinic prescribe medicines requiring ongoing monitoring or a risk-management programme (e.g. MHRA pregnancy-prevention requirements, baseline bloods, ECG)? **Default: NO** — unless the monitoring arrangements, responsible person and recall method are documented in an appendix to this policy.]`

### Where medicines for long-term conditions are prescribed
`[CONFIRM: Does your clinic initiate or continue medicines for long-term conditions? **Default: NO — acute and episodic care only; long-term-condition management remains with the patient's own GP.** If yes, document who is responsible for ongoing monitoring, the review interval, and how the patient is recalled.]`

### Keeping current
Staff have access to the current BNF, MIMS and NICE guidance. Prescribing clinicians receive **MHRA Drug Safety Updates**, and **National Patient Safety Alerts issued through the Central Alerting System** are acted on promptly and their action recorded (see C07e Managing Safety Alerts).

### Disposal
Medicines returned to the clinic — including those of a deceased patient — are disposed of through an authorised route.
`[CONFIRM: What is your clinic's disposal route? **Default: returned to a registered pharmacy for destruction; the return is recorded.**]`

### Records
Every prescribing decision — including the indication, the information given, the patient's decisions (including any refusal of GP notification), and the review or follow-up arranged — is recorded in the clinical record at the time (see C09 Patient Record Keeping).

## What changed from the 2022 original — and why

**De-personalised (facts → placeholders, process text kept verbatim where sound)**
- "Martha Walker" (author) → Clinickly governed template
- "Registered Manager – Faheem Ahmed" → `[CONFIRM: named responsible person]`
- "this practice/establishment" → `[CLINIC NAME]`
- "returned to the Pharmacy for destruction" — Ahmeys-specific (a pharmacy next door) → `[CONFIRM: disposal route]` with a sensible default
- "This practice does not prescribe controlled drugs" — a **clinic decision**, not a fact → `[CONFIRM: … Default: NO]`

**Errors corrected**
- ❗ **"MIMMS" → "MIMS".** MIMMS is *Major Incident Medical Management and Support* — a completely different thing. The original cited the wrong publication three times.
- ❗ **"National Patient Safety Agency" → National Patient Safety Alerts via the Central Alerting System.** The NPSA was **abolished in 2012** — the 2022 policy referenced a body that hadn't existed for a decade.
- GMC reference updated to *Good practice in prescribing and managing medicines and devices* (2021).

**Added — because the CQC inspector actually asked about them**
- **Formulary / restricted prescribing** — she asked *"which of the policies are those local guidelines outlined in?"*
- **Follow-up for long-term-condition medicines** — she asked directly; the original was silent, and she said so: *"the policies don't say as such"*
- **Prescription without same-day consultation** — probed via repeat prescribing
- **What happens when the patient refuses GP notification** — the original said "consider whether to prescribe"; now requires the decision and reasoning to be recorded
- **Remote prescribing standards** — she asked about online consultations; the original didn't mention them
- **Monitoring/risk-management programmes** (e.g. MHRA pregnancy prevention)
- **Prescribing within competence and declared scope** — GPhC Standard 5

**Structure**
- Full house format restored: document control · references · scope · AIM → POLICY → PROCEDURE → END
- Regulator mapping added (themes 6 & 7; CQC Reg 12; GPhC Std 4/5 and Principle 4)
- Review date now **set on sign-off** rather than left blank
