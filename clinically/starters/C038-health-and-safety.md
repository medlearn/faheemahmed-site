## POLICY

`[CLINIC NAME]` is committed to ensuring the health, safety and welfare of its employees and will, so far as is reasonably practicable, establish the procedures and systems necessary to implement that commitment and comply with its statutory obligations.

**Health and safety at work is the responsibility of every employee**, including taking reasonable care of their own and others' health and safety, and reporting any situation that may pose a serious or imminent threat.

> **If you are unsure how to perform a task because of insufficient training, or believe it would be dangerous to perform a job or use a piece of equipment, it is your duty to report it to `[CONFIRM: role]` immediately, explaining why.** You may also raise it under the grievance procedure, or under **S03 Speaking Up**.

Particular attention is paid to:

- Maintaining the workplace in a safe condition, with adequate welfare facilities
- Safe means of **access to and egress from** the workplace
- Provision and maintenance of safe equipment and systems of work
- Safe use, handling, storage and transport of items and substances
- Information, instruction, training and supervision sufficient for employees to work safely

Disciplinary action may be taken against any employee who violates health and safety rules or fails to perform their duties under health and safety legislation. Depending on seriousness this may amount to gross misconduct and summary dismissal.

## What changed from C038 v01 — and why

v01 was a sound general statement of health and safety commitment. Its opening — that health and safety is everyone's responsibility, that an employee unsure how to perform a task safely has a **duty** to say so, and that breaches may amount to gross misconduct — is well judged and retained.

Three changes.

### 1. One person held every safety role

v01's responsibilities section reads, in full:

> *`<Faheem Ahmed>` – overall responsibility for health and safety.*
> *`<Faheem Ahmed>` – fire warden.*
> *`<Faheem Ahmed>` – fire warden.*
> *`<Faheem Ahmed>` – first aider.*

The same individual is named four times, including **twice as fire warden** — which suggests a template with two fire warden slots, both completed with the same name rather than one being left blank.

The practical consequence: **when that person is on leave, off sick or off site, the clinic has no fire warden and no first aider.** That is precisely when an incident is hardest to manage, and it is a visible single point of failure across every safety role at once.

v02 requires a named deputy for each, with a `[CONFIRM]` acknowledging that a very small clinic may genuinely be unable to separate them — in which case it must state what happens when the single post-holder is absent. *"One person holds every role"* is not a plan. *"One person holds every role, and here is what we do when they are away"* is.

The policy also leaves **`<INSERT NAME>`** unreplaced as the person to whom obstructed access or egress is reported — one of the six unfinished template fields found across the corpus.

### 2. Four required areas were missing entirely

A search of v01 returns **zero** matches for each of:

| | |
|---|---|
| **COSHH** | Required wherever hazardous substances are used. The clinic uses **10,000 ppm chlorine-releasing agents** for blood spills, which C02 correctly warns must be used under COSHH — but no COSHH assessment or register is required anywhere |
| **Lone working** | A real and likely risk in a small clinic — someone opening early, closing late, or consulting when nobody else is on site |
| **Display screen equipment** | A specific regulatory duty |
| **Violence and aggression toward staff** | The health and safety risk most likely to actually materialise in a clinic. v01 protects staff from equipment and substances but not from people |

**Employers' liability insurance** display was also absent, and the **Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999** — the source of the risk assessment duty the policy relies on — was not cited.

Fire is mentioned, but the **fire risk assessment** appears once with no owner, date or review cycle, and there is no schedule for alarm tests, drills or escape route checks. Given the premises are leased, v02 adds the same principle applied to cleaning in C02: **where the landlord holds it, obtain a copy — responsibility does not transfer with the task.**

### 3. References

*"Principles of H&S at Work Act 1974"* is not the title of the Act; it is the **Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974**. Beyond that Act and a link to the HSE website, **no regulations were cited at all** — not the Management Regulations, the Fire Safety Order, COSHH, the First-Aid Regulations, the DSE Regulations, Manual Handling, or the Electricity at Work Regulations.

RIDDOR and portable appliance testing do appear in the body of v01, which is more than several other policies manage.

### Additions

- **Calibration of clinical equipment** — defibrillator, oxygen, BP monitors, scales, fridge thermometers. A Regulation 15 expectation and a common inspection gap
- **Evacuation of patients with limited mobility**, including personal emergency evacuation plans
- **The distinction between the workplace first aid kit and clinical emergency equipment** — they serve different purposes and a clinic needs both. See C034
- A **first aid needs assessment** rather than a fixed number of first aiders, since the required level depends on the risks
