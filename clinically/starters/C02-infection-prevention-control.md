## POLICY

1. **The premises are clean and fit for purpose, and all staff maintain hand hygiene and personal infection control.**

2. **A named IPC Lead is accountable for infection prevention and control.**

   | | |
   |---|---|
   | **IPC Lead** | `[CONFIRM: NAMED individual]` |
   | **Deputy** | `[CONFIRM: NAMED individual]` |

   The IPC Lead ensures checks are carried out, maintains the IPC folder, leads the annual audit programme, and reports to `[CONFIRM: the governance meeting]`.

3. **Standard precautions apply to every patient, every time.** We do not always know which patients carry organisms that can cause infection, so precautions are not applied selectively:

   - Hand hygiene
   - Appropriate use of gloves
   - Appropriate use of aprons and other PPE
   - Appropriate handling and disposal of waste and sharps
   - Appropriate handling of clean and used linen
   - Keeping the environment free of clutter for ease of cleaning
   - Appropriate decontamination of equipment, per IPC principles and manufacturer's instructions
   - Managing patients with transmissible infection — see below

4. **Outsourcing cleaning does not outsource responsibility.** Where cleaning is provided by a landlord or contractor, `[CLINIC NAME]` remains accountable under Regulations 12 and 15 for the standard achieved. `[CONFIRM: see the assurance requirements below — the clinic must hold its own evidence, not rely on the landlord holding it.]`

5. **Every IPC breach is reported** as an incident under C017.

## PROCEDURE

### Hand hygiene and skin protection

All staff are trained in hand hygiene at induction and `[CONFIRM: default annually]`.

Liquid soap and water, using the **Ayliffe technique** — the poster is displayed at every clinical hand-wash basin:

- **Before and after** all direct contact with patients, their immediate environment and contaminated items
- After removing PPE, especially after glove removal and after touching a face mask
- Before carrying out an aseptic technique
- After using the toilet, before handling food, and on entering and leaving clinical areas

All cuts and abrasions are covered with a **waterproof dressing**. Alcohol hand gel is available at the entrance and in clinical areas.

> **Alcohol gel is not effective against all organisms.** Where a patient has diarrhoea or vomiting, or a spore-forming organism is suspected, **soap and water must be used** — gel is not a substitute.

`[CONFIRM: bare below the elbows policy in clinical areas. Recommended: yes — short sleeves, no wrist watch, no stoned rings, nails short and free of polish or extensions.]`

### PPE

Available for use as appropriate: gloves · aprons · gowns · fluid-resistant surgical masks · eye protection or face shields · hair covers.

| Item | When |
|---|---|
| **Gloves** | Contact with blood, body fluids, secretions, excretions, contaminated items; before touching mucous membranes or non-intact skin. **Changed between patients and, where indicated, between procedures on the same patient.** Gloves are not a substitute for hand hygiene — hands are washed after removal |
| **Aprons** | Direct patient contact, handling contaminated items, or where splashing is anticipated. **Changed between patients**, after a 'dirty' procedure, and before an aseptic technique |
| **Masks and eye protection** | Where contamination of the eyes or mucous membranes with blood or body fluids is likely. Also where a patient has a productive cough |
| **Respiratory protection** | `[CONFIRM: what is held, and when it is used — see respiratory infections below]` |

Soiled PPE is disposed of as clinical waste.

`[CONFIRM: who checks PPE stock levels and how often. Default: IPC Lead, monthly — a policy requiring PPE that is not in the cupboard is not a control.]`

### Respiratory infections and transmissible illness

> **This section was absent from v01.**

`[CONFIRM: the clinic's approach. The defaults below are a reasonable starting position for a small outpatient clinic and should be reviewed against current UKHSA guidance.]`

- Patients with symptoms of a transmissible respiratory infection are asked, at booking and on arrival, to say so
- **Where the appointment can safely be deferred or conducted remotely, it is** — this is the primary control in a clinic with no isolation facilities
- Where a patient with respiratory symptoms must be seen: they are offered a surgical mask, seen promptly rather than kept in the waiting area, and seen in a room that can be ventilated and cleaned afterwards
- Staff wear appropriate PPE `[CONFIRM: what]`
- The room is cleaned after use, with attention to touch points
- **Staff with symptoms of a transmissible infection do not attend work.** `[CONFIRM: exclusion periods — particularly diarrhoea and vomiting: 48 hours symptom-free is the standard]`
- `[CONFIRM: ventilation arrangements in clinical rooms — natural, mechanical, or neither. Where a room cannot be ventilated, that is a risk to record and manage, not to ignore]`

**Isolation is not practicable in most small clinics.** Where a patient needs isolation, the honest control is to defer, redirect, or see them at the end of the session — not to write "isolate the patient" into a policy the premises cannot deliver.

### Sharps

- Dispose of sharps into a **sharps bin at the point of use**. Never carry an unsheathed sharp
- **Do not re-sheath needles** unless there is a safe means of doing so
- Use **safer sharps devices** where reasonably practicable — this is a requirement of the Health and Safety (Sharp Instruments in Healthcare) Regulations 2013, not a preference
- Sharps bins are assembled correctly, labelled with the date and the assembler, kept off the floor and out of public reach, closed at the temporary closure between uses, and **never filled above the fill line**
- The person who uses the sharp is responsible for disposing of it

### Sharps injury and exposure to blood-borne viruses

**Immediate actions:**

1. **Gently encourage bleeding — do not suck the area**
2. Wash with soap and water — **do not scrub**
3. Rinse mucous membranes with warm water — **do not swallow**
4. Cover broken skin with a waterproof dressing

**Then:**

5. **Report immediately** to `[CONFIRM: role]` — do not wait until the end of the session
6. Assess whether the exposure is **significant**: percutaneous injury; exposure of broken skin; exposure of mucous membranes including the eye
7. Where significant, seek urgent occupational health or A&E advice — **post-exposure prophylaxis is time-critical and its effectiveness falls with delay.** `[CONFIRM: the named route and out-of-hours arrangement. This must be a real service the clinic has arranged, reachable at the time it is needed]`
8. Report as an incident under C017
9. **Consider RIDDOR.** A sharps injury from a contaminated sharp is reportable to the HSE as a **dangerous occurrence**, and a resulting infection is reportable as a disease. `[CONFIRM: who makes RIDDOR reports]`

See Appendix 1.

### Waste

- Dispose of waste promptly into the correct bag or container — see C06
- Bags and containers no more than **three-quarters full**
- Sealed with a traceable tag before removal to the disposal hold area; identification labels on rigid containers completed
- `[CONFIRM: waste contractor, collection frequency, and where consignment notes are held. Consignment notes are the evidence — the clinic keeps its own copies]`

### Spillages

Blood spills, or spillages of body fluid stained with blood, are cleaned immediately using a **10,000 ppm chlorine-releasing agent** or granules, with appropriate PPE.

**Under COSHH: do not use chlorine-releasing agents in a confined space, or directly onto spills of urine or vomit.** A spillage kit is available. `[CONFIRM: where it is kept and who checks it.]`

### Linen and soft furnishings

`[CONFIRM: applicable only where the clinic uses reusable linen. Where everything is disposable, say so.]`

- **Couch roll and disposables — including pillow and modesty covers — are changed after each patient**
- Never place used linen on the floor; use the correct colour-coded bag
- Roll or fold used items carefully to prevent airborne dispersal of skin cells
- Infected linen goes into a water-soluble bag inside a waterproof outer bag
- Curtains and screens are changed if visibly soiled, after use by a patient with a transmissible infection, and routinely **at least every six months** `[CONFIRM: who tracks this and where it is recorded]`

### Equipment and decontamination

- **Single-use (disposable) instruments are used wherever possible.** A single-use item is never reprocessed
- Any equipment used for more than one patient — **including a stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, pulse oximeter or thermometer** — is decontaminated between patients
- Decontamination means cleaning, and may be followed by disinfection or sterilisation depending on the item and its use
- **Manufacturer's instructions are followed** and kept accessible
- `[CONFIRM: whether any reusable instruments are decontaminated on the premises. Default: NO. Where YES, a full decontamination procedure, validated equipment, and HTM-compliant records are required, and that is a substantially larger undertaking than this policy covers]`

**Equipment leaving the premises for repair or servicing** — see Appendix 2. It must be cleaned, carry a completed decontamination certificate, be bagged where appropriate, and be logged.

### Environment and cleaning

- Surfaces are kept free of clutter for ease of cleaning
- **A cleaning schedule specifies what is cleaned, how often, with what, and by whom**, covering clinical and non-clinical areas
- **Cleaning logs are completed and retained**, and the IPC Lead checks them

## RESPONSIBILITIES

**All staff** are personally accountable for complying with this policy. Everyone has a legal duty to take reasonable care of their own health and safety and that of others, and to report incidents and concerns.

**Clinical staff** are responsible for identifying infectious conditions and circumstances that may lead to transmission, and for notifying the IPC Lead.

**Any breach of this policy** places staff, patients and visitors at risk and requires completion of a significant event form under C017.

## What changed from C02 v01 — and why

v01 was practically sound on the clinical basics — Ayliffe technique, glove and apron use, sharps handling, spillage management, the BBV exposure flowchart and the decontamination-certificate procedure are all correct and are retained largely as written. Five changes.

### 1. It contained nothing on respiratory infection — in March 2022

A search of v01 for *COVID*, *coronavirus*, *respiratory*, *ventilation*, *legionella* and *RIDDOR* returns **zero matches**.

The only reference to a coughing patient is that staff should wear a fluid shield mask *"if the patient has a severe cough and is not compliant with instruction to cover their mouth"* — which frames respiratory precautions as a response to an uncooperative patient rather than as a control.

By the date of this policy, respiratory infection management, ventilation, patient triage before attendance and staff exclusion were standard components of any healthcare IPC policy. Their complete absence is the clearest evidence that the document was assembled from a pre-2020 template.

### 2. One line contradicts the clinic's own model

> *"Only the doctors come into clinical contact with patients at Ahmeys Clinic."*

This appears in the staff immunisation section, and it does not describe a pharmacist-led prescribing service — particularly one whose **named IPC Lead is a pharmacist**. Taken at face value it would exclude the clinic's own prescribers from the Hepatitis B requirement.

This is the same category of error as the C032 under-18s contradiction: a factual statement about how the clinic operates, wrong, embedded where it drives a control.

### 3. Responsibility for cleaning was delegated to the landlord along with the paperwork

v01 states that cleaning is provided under a service level agreement with the landlord, and:

> *"The landlord holds all relevant cleaning documents / contracts."*

Outsourcing the work is entirely legitimate. **Outsourcing the assurance is not.** `[CLINIC NAME]` remains responsible under Regulations 12 and 15 for the cleanliness of its own consulting rooms, and an inspector will ask the clinic — not the landlord — to evidence it. v02 lists what the clinic must hold copies of, and adds a quarterly joint review.

Added alongside: **water safety and legionella**, which follow the same pattern — commonly a landlord responsibility, and commonly missing from the tenant's own evidence.

### 4. It was written for an inpatient setting, and for domiciliary care

v01 refers to *"working around the patient's bed"*, dealing with linen *"at the bedside"*, *"after a patient with an infectious condition has been discharged"*, bed screens, sheets — and, in the spillage section, *"in the patients' own home use detergent and water."*

`[CLINIC NAME]` has no beds and does not visit homes. Retained where it translates (couch roll, curtains, disposables), removed where it does not.

### 5. References

- The **Hygiene Code** is cited in its **2011** edition; it has been revised since
- *Infection Prevention and Control Guidance and Protocols for Primary Medical Care* is cited as **April 2014**
- The BBV flowchart is referenced to **Grampian NHS Occupational Health Service** — a Scottish service, for a clinic in England
- **"Rosalie Thompson – infection control nurse"** is listed as a reference. A named individual is a source of advice, not a citable reference — and if that person is no longer involved, the policy cites something that no longer exists
- Neither the **Sharps Regulations 2013** nor **RIDDOR** was cited

### Additions

- **A named deputy IPC Lead**
- **Safer sharps devices** — a legal requirement under the 2013 Regulations, absent from v01
- **RIDDOR reporting of sharps injuries** — a contaminated sharps injury is a reportable dangerous occurrence
- **Alcohol gel is not effective against everything** — soap and water for diarrhoea, vomiting and spore-forming organisms. A genuinely common and consequential error
- **Sharps bin management** — assembly, labelling, dating, fill line, temporary closure — none of which was in v01
- **PPE stock checks.** A policy requiring PPE that is not in the cupboard is not a control
- **An annual IPC statement**, which is what the Hygiene Code expects and what an inspector asks for first
- **Hand hygiene audit by observation**, not self-report
- **Honesty about isolation.** v01 lists *"isolation of patients with certain infections"* among standard precautions. Most small clinics cannot isolate anyone. The real control is to defer, redirect, or see the patient at the end of the session — and writing that down is more useful than writing down something the premises cannot deliver
