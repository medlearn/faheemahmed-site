## Notes for the clinician

**Consent for photography is separate from consent to treatment.** A patient agreeing to be examined has not agreed to be photographed.

**There are three different consents here, and they are not the same.** A patient may agree to one and refuse another:

| | |
|---|---|
| **A — Clinical care** | The image forms part of their record, to track their own condition |
| **B — Professional education** | Anonymised discussion with colleagues, teaching, MDT |
| **C — Publication or marketing** | Website, social media, printed material, before-and-after |

**Never treat A as implying B, or B as implying C.**

**Before taking the image:**

- A **face is not anonymisable.** Do not photograph one for educational use
- Check the frame — tattoos, jewellery, name bands, documents, screens, other people, distinctive scars
- Photograph the smallest area that shows what matters
- `[CONFIRM: which device may be used. Default: clinic-owned equipment only. Never a personal phone]`

**Consent can be withdrawn at any time.** Before taking the image, be sure you could find and delete it later if asked.

## What we are asking

**Please read this, and tick only what you agree to.** You can agree to some and not others. You can say no to all of it.

---

### A · For my own care ☐

The image goes in my medical record, so my clinician can compare how my condition changes over time.

*Only the people involved in my care will see it.*

---

### B · For professional education ☐

The image may be shown to other healthcare professionals for teaching and learning — including anonymised case discussion with a specialist panel.

**What this means:**

- My name, date of birth and contact details are **never** attached to the image
- Only my approximate age range and a description of the condition go with it
- It will not show my face
- The professionals who see it are bound by the same duty of confidentiality as my own clinician
- It is **not** made public

**What we cannot promise:** if you have a distinctive feature in the image — a tattoo, a birthmark, a scar — someone who knows you might recognise it. Tell us if you are worried, and we can crop it, photograph a smaller area, or not take it at all.

---

### C · For publication or marketing ☐

The image may be used in published material — the clinic website, social media, printed leaflets, presentations or teaching materials that are publicly available.

> ⚠️ **Please think carefully about this one.**
>
> **Once an image is published we may not be able to withdraw it.** It may have been copied, saved or shared by others. You can ask us to remove it from our own material and we will, but we cannot recover copies elsewhere.
>
> **You do not have to agree to this**, and your care will be exactly the same either way. Nobody here will ask you again if you say no.

`[CONFIRM: delete section C entirely where the clinic does not use images for marketing. Do not present an option you do not intend to use — and never make agreeing to C a condition of treatment, or attach any discount or benefit to it.]`

## Your rights

- **You can say no.** Your care will not be affected in any way
- **You can change your mind at any time.** Tell any member of staff, or contact us using the details above. We will stop using the image and delete it, except where it has already been published (section C)
- **You do not have to give a reason**
- **You can ask to see the image** before agreeing
- **You can ask us to delete it** at any time
