# If something goes wrong

## `[CLINIC NAME]`

---

We want you to have a good experience here. Sometimes things go wrong, or fall short of what you expected. **When that happens, we want to know.**

**Telling us will never affect the care you receive.** You will not be treated differently, moved down a waiting list, or refused an appointment because you have complained. If you are worried about that, please say so — we would rather know.

---

## Just tell someone

**Most things are sorted out quickly, on the spot.** If something isn't right, tell any member of staff. Often they can fix it there and then.

If they can't, they will involve someone who can, the same day.

---

## If you want to take it further

**You can complain in whatever way suits you** — in person, by phone, by email or by letter. You don't need to use a form or particular words.

| | When |
|---|---|
| **We will acknowledge your complaint** | Within **`[CONFIRM: 2 working days]`** |

| **If we need longer, we will tell you why and give you a new date** | **Before** the deadline, not after |

> `[CONFIRM: these three figures must be identical to the timescales table in C04.]`

**Our response will tell you:** what we looked into, what we found — **including where we got it wrong** — an apology if one is owed, and **what we are changing as a result**.

**If you would like to meet us**, we can arrange that. You are welcome to bring someone with you.

---

## What this process is not for

This procedure does not deal with legal liability or compensation. It is about finding out what happened, putting it right where we can, and changing what we do.

**Complaining does not affect your right to take legal action.**

## If your complaint is about a bill

**Please raise it the same way.** Fee disputes go through this same process, with the same timescales and the same right to take it further. We don't handle money complaints informally or off the record.

---

## If something went wrong with your care

If something has gone wrong and affected you, **we will tell you about it, explain what happened and apologise — whether or not you have complained.** That is a duty we have, not a favour.
