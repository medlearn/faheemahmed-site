## What changed from C015 v01 — and why

v01 was **388 words**, and it is the policy that describes what the clinic *is*. Its principles were right — a clear structure, clear lines of responsibility, staff signing to confirm they have read relevant policies — and are retained.

The problem is that it described almost nothing.

### 1. The entire service description was one line

v01's *Services Provided* section reads, in full:

> *"Ahmeys Clinic sees a range of patients including, but not limited to:*
> *• Those with minor illnesses between 9 am-4.30 pm"*

That is the whole thing. No age range, no conditions, no list of what the clinic does not do, no out-of-hours arrangement, and *"including, but not limited to"* which makes even the one item non-binding.

**This is the document an inspector reads first**, and the one they compare against the Statement of Purpose and against what they can see happening. v02 replaces it with a structured description that has to be completed.

The most important addition is **what the clinic does not do**, and where those patients go instead. Scope is defined by its edges.

### 2. It named a management structure that does not exist

v01 refers to *"the national and compliance managers"*, *"the Registered Manager/Chief Executive"*, *"the Medical Director"*, and patients assessed *"by the Consultant"*.

A small pharmacist-led clinic has no national manager, no chief executive, no medical director and no consultant. This is the same pattern found throughout the corpus — a template built for a larger organisation, adopted without substituting the roles that actually exist.

v02 replaces it with a table of the **governance roles this clinic genuinely needs someone to hold** — registered manager, clinical lead, safeguarding, IPC, medicines, risk, complaints, IG — because those are the ones every other policy in the set refers back to.

It also adds the `[CONFIRM]` that matters most in a small clinic: **where one person holds several of these roles, who is the alternative when a concern involves them.**

### 3. Governance meetings were mentioned but not defined

v01 says the Clinical Governance Committee *"meets regularly"*. Not what it is called, who attends, how often, what it discusses, or that minutes are kept.

Nearly every other policy in this set ends by reporting something to "the governance meeting". If that meeting is not defined anywhere, none of those reporting lines are real. v02 defines it, with a standing agenda drawn from what the other policies send to it.

### 4. Out-of-hours was absent

v01 gives opening hours of 9am–4.30pm and says nothing about what happens outside them.

For a service running **titration-based treatment**, this is a live safety question: a patient who has just started a new medicine and becomes unwell at 8pm needs to know where to go. v02 makes it an explicit `[CONFIRM]`, including the case where the honest answer is that there is no out-of-hours cover — in which case patients must be told that at the outset, along with exactly what to do instead.
