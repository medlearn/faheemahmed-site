## POLICY

1. **Nobody works unsupervised in a role for which they have not been assessed as competent.**

2. **Mandatory training is mandatory.** It is a condition of working here, not an optional development opportunity. `[CONFIRM: what happens where mandatory training lapses. Default: the individual is removed from the relevant duties until it is completed.]`

3. **Training is aligned to the needs of the patient population** and to current **NICE** guidance and relevant Royal College and professional body guidance.

4. **Everyone has an induction, ongoing supervision and an annual appraisal** — including those working under practising privileges and on a sessional basis.

5. **Every registrant is responsible for their own registration, revalidation and CPD.** The clinic supports it; it does not own it.

6. **A Staff Training Matrix records what each person needs, when they last did it, and when it is next due.** `[CONFIRM: who holds and maintains the matrix.]`

### Role-specific and clinical training

All clinical staff receive training relevant to the conditions they manage, taking account of **NICE** guidance and guidance from the relevant Royal College or professional body.

This includes:

- Meeting the needs of patients with physical and/or learning disabilities
- The management of the specific conditions the clinic treats
- `[CONFIRM: service-specific training — e.g. for an ADHD service: diagnostic frameworks, titration protocols, cardiovascular monitoring, controlled drugs handling]`

**Training needs are identified from:** audit outcomes · incidents and near misses · complaints · new treatments or services · appraisal · patient feedback · changes in guidance or law.

### Induction

Every new worker, including locums and agency staff, completes an induction before working unsupervised. `[CONFIRM: who delivers it and over what period.]`

Induction covers, as a minimum: the clinic and its services · their role and scope · **safeguarding and how to raise a concern** · confidentiality and IG · incident reporting · complaints · health and safety, fire and emergency procedures · infection control · where policies are held and the requirement to read those relevant to their role.

**Induction is recorded and signed by both parties.**

## What changed from C010 v01 — and why

v01 was **349 words** — the shortest policy in the corpus, and the one carrying **Regulation 18**, the staffing regulation. Its core principle was right and is retained: training must be aligned to the needs of the patient population and to NICE and Royal College guidance.

Four changes.

### 1. It stated that clinical training is not mandatory

> *"Though **not considered a part of the mandatory training programme**, access to relevant training will be provided as a matter of importance to the appropriate staff in the appropriate circumstances."*

Training in the management of the conditions a clinician treats is not a discretionary development opportunity — it is the basis of competence under Regulation 18, and under **GPhC Standard 4** and GMC guidance it is a personal professional obligation. As written, the sentence tells staff that condition-specific training is optional and available on request.

### 2. There was no mandatory training list at all

v01 refers to a *"mandatory training programme"* without saying what is in it, who needs what, or how often. There was no list, no frequencies, and no consequence for lapse.

Everything an inspector asks about staffing starts here: *show me the training matrix; is anyone out of date; what did you do about it.* v02 provides a starting list of fifteen items with `[CONFIRM]` frequencies.

### 3. Supervision, appraisal and induction were absent

The policy is called *Managing Patient Conditions Staff Training*, and it covers only training. **Regulation 18 requires that staff receive appropriate support, training, professional development, supervision and appraisal.** Three of those five were missing.

Also missing: how competence is assessed before someone practises independently — which is not the same thing as having attended a course.

### 4. Registration and revalidation were not mentioned

For a clinic staffed by registrants, verifying registration and supporting revalidation is a core staffing control. A lapsed registration means someone is practising unlawfully, and the clinic — not only the individual — carries that.

v02 adds verification at recruitment and annually, indemnity checks, and the actual requirements of each regulator — including the **GPhC's six records** model, which counts records rather than hours and which nothing in the corpus referenced.
